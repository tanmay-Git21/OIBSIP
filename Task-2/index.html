Bhagat Singh

A tribute to a revolutionary hero of India

Bhagat Singh (1907–1931) was an Indian revolutionary freedom fighter who played a significant role in India's struggle for independence. Known for his bravery, intelligence, and unwavering patriotism, he inspired millions of Indians with his selfless sacrifices. Bhagat Singh's legacy lives on, and he remains a symbol of courage and resistance against oppression.

Early Life

Born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, Bhagat Singh was deeply influenced by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and other brutal actions of the British colonial rulers. His family was actively involved in the freedom movement, which fueled his revolutionary spirit from a young age.

Revolutionary Activities

Bhagat Singh joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association and became involved in various revolutionary activities aimed at fighting the oppressive British regime. His famous slogan, "Inquilab Zindabad!" (Long Live the Revolution!), still resonates as a powerful cry for justice.

Legacy

Despite his young age, Bhagat Singh’s ideas and actions have left a lasting impact on Indian history. His ultimate sacrifice, along with fellow freedom fighters Rajguru and Sukhdev, continues to inspire generations to value freedom and justice.

Dedicated to the memory of Bhagat Singh - a true patriot and a fearless revolutionary.
